Welcome to the Embarcadero Dev-C++ User Manual!

The Embarcadero Dev-C++ user manual has undergone a complete overhaul and significant expansion as of version 5.5.2. Our primary aim with this redevelopment is to provide a truly comprehensive and easy-to-navigate resource for all users, regardless of their experience level. Whether you're just starting your journey into C++ programming or you're a seasoned developer looking for specific functionalities, this manual is designed to be your go-to guide. We've dedicated considerable effort to clarify complex topics, introduce new features, and organize information in a more intuitive manner.

However, we want to be upfront: this manual is still a dynamic and evolving document, far from its final form. We firmly believe that the best documentation is a living one, constantly improved and refined through community collaboration. Your insights and contributions are not just welcome, they are absolutely crucial to making this manual the best it can be. If you identify areas that could benefit from clearer explanations, spot gaps in our coverage, or even have ideas for entirely new sections or tutorials that would assist other users, please don't hesitate to share them. You can create new pages or suggest edits and send them directly to us. Every single contribution, no matter how small, plays a vital role in enhancing this resource for the entire Dev-C++ community. Alternatively, if you find this manual exceptionally helpful and wish to support its ongoing development and maintenance, financial donations are also greatly appreciated and help us dedicate more resources to its improvement.

To begin your exploration of the manual's extensive content, simply utilize the **tree view** located on the left side of your screen. This intuitive, hierarchical navigation system allows you to easily browse through the various sections, chapters, and sub-topics, ensuring you can quickly pinpoint the information you need. Each section is designed to be modular, allowing you to jump directly to relevant areas without having to read through unnecessary introductory material. We've also incorporated internal links where appropriate to help you cross-reference related information seamlessly.

Compatibility and Viewing Experience

We understand that users access our documentation through a variety of web browsers, and ensuring a consistent and optimal viewing experience is a top priority. This help file has been thoroughly tested across a range of popular browsers to guarantee its display and functionality are as expected. Our goal is to minimize any technical hurdles, allowing you to focus on the content itself.

Here's a detailed breakdown of our compatibility testing results:

  • Firefox: We've conducted extensive testing on versions **24.0 and 25.0**, and we're pleased to confirm that the manual works flawlessly. You can expect all features, formatting, and interactive elements to perform perfectly.
  • Chrome: Similarly, **Chrome versions 29.x and 30.x** have demonstrated full compatibility and excellent rendering. The manual displays beautifully and responsively on these browser versions.
  • Internet Explorer: For users of Microsoft's browser, **versions 9 and 10** are fully functional. All content is accessible, and the layout is rendered as intended, providing a reliable experience.
  • Internet Explorer 8: While the core content of the manual remains fully functional on **Internet Explorer 8**, users might notice some minor cosmetic inconsistencies or display quirks. These are generally superficial and do not impede access to the information, but for the best visual experience, we recommend using a more modern browser.

We continuously monitor browser updates and strive to maintain broad compatibility. For the most optimal and visually appealing viewing experience, we highly recommend using one of the fully supported, modern web browsers such as the latest versions of Firefox or Chrome. This ensures you benefit from the most up-to-date rendering engines and web standards, which our manual is designed to leverage.
